Provinces of Canada

Ontario

Capital: Toronto

Population: 14.57 million (2021)

Area: 1,076,395 km²

Ontario is Canada's most populous province, known for its vibrant cities, beautiful parks, and significant cultural and economic contributions.

Quebec

Capital: Quebec City

Population: 8.48 million (2021)

Area: 1,542,056 km²

Quebec is the largest province by area and is predominantly French-speaking. It has a unique culture and history, with famous cities like Montreal and Quebec City.

British Columbia

Capital: Victoria

Population: 5.07 million (2021)

Area: 944,735 km²

British Columbia is known for its stunning natural landscapes, including mountains, forests, and coastlines. Vancouver, its largest city, is a major hub for technology and film.

Alberta

Capital: Edmonton

Population: 4.44 million (2021)

Area: 661,848 km²

Alberta is famous for its natural resources, including oil and gas. It also boasts beautiful national parks like Banff and Jasper.

Manitoba

Capital: Winnipeg

Population: 1.38 million (2021)

Area: 649,950 km²

Manitoba is known for its diverse culture and natural beauty, including numerous lakes and rivers. Winnipeg is its largest city and cultural center.

Saskatchewan

Capital: Regina

Population: 1.17 million (2021)

Area: 651,036 km²

Saskatchewan is a prairie province known for its agriculture, especially wheat. It also has vibrant cities like Saskatoon and Regina.

Nova Scotia

Capital: Halifax

Population: 1.01 million (2021)

Area: 55,284 km²

Nova Scotia is a maritime province known for its coastline, seafood, and rich history. Halifax is a major cultural and economic center in the region.

New Brunswick

Capital: Fredericton

Population: 789,225 (2021)

Area: 72,908 km²

New Brunswick is known for its forests, river valleys, and cultural diversity, including both English and French-speaking communities.

Newfoundland and Labrador

Capital: St. John's

Population: 521,365 (2021)

Area: 405,212 km²

Newfoundland and Labrador is known for its rugged coastline, maritime culture, and rich history as one of the first areas of North America to be settled by Europeans.

Prince Edward Island

Capital: Charlottetown

Population: 164,318 (2021)

Area: 5,660 km²

Prince Edward Island is Canada's smallest province, known for its red sand beaches, lighthouses, and as the setting for the famous novel "Anne of Green Gables."
